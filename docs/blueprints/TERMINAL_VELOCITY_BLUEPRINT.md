## PREMISE

This is not a report. This is an autopsy.

We are not studying "sustainability challenges" or "environmental issues." We are documenting the mechanisms by which an industrial civilization is accelerating toward hard planetary boundaries with full knowledge of the consequences and apparent inability to change course.

The question is not *whether* these systems are destructiveâ€”that debate ended decades ago. The question is *how*: What are the specific causal chains? Where are the feedback loops? Who benefits from continuation? What would it actually take to interrupt the mechanism?

Every investigation in this project follows the same logic a medical examiner uses:

1. **Observe the damage** (what's dying, what's degraded, what's gone)
2. **Trace the wound channel** (what caused the damage, through what pathway)
3. **Identify the weapon** (the specific human activity, system, or incentive)
4. **Profile the perpetrator** (who benefits, who decides, who could stop it)
5. **Assess intervention difficulty** (what would it take to prevent recurrence)

We are not advocates. We are not activists. We are coroners.

## VOICE & TONE PRINCIPLES

### What We Sound Like

**Clinical precision with controlled fury.** The tone is a trauma surgeon who has seen this injury beforeâ€”professional, competent, unsentimental, but with an undercurrent of moral clarity that doesn't need to announce itself.

**Specificity over abstraction.** Never "pollution is bad." Always "this facility released 47,000 kg of benzene in 2023, exceeding its permit by 340%, resulting in blood cancer clusters within 2km radius documented by [source]."

**Show the math, skip the sermon.** The numbers are the argument. If the evidence doesn't speak for itself, we haven't found the right evidence yet.

**Name names.** Systems don't make decisions. People do. Corporations have executives. Agencies have directors. Policies have authors. We identify them.

**Acknowledge uncertainty like adults.** "We don't know" is a valid finding. "The data doesn't exist" is an indictment. "Studies conflict" is a starting point, not an excuse.

### What We Don't Sound Like

- Academic hedge-speak ("further research is needed to fully understand...")
- Activist hyperbole ("the planet is literally on fire")
- Corporate sustainability theater ("committed to net-zero by 2050")
- Techno-optimist denial ("innovation will solve this")
- Doomer paralysis ("nothing can be done")

### The Signature Line

Every major output ends with the same line:

> **Filed under: Causes of Death, Preventable**

## EVIDENCE GRADING SYSTEM

Borrowed from clinical medicine's evidence hierarchy, adapted for sustainability research.

### Evidence Tiers

| Grade | Name | Definition | Use |
|-------|------|------------|-----|
| **I** | Direct Measurement | Empirical observations from peer-reviewed monitoring systems, experiments, or validated sensor networks | Primary basis for damage quantification |
| **II** | Official Inventory | Government or multilateral agency accounting (emissions inventories, census data, trade statistics) | Baseline metrics, trend analysis |
| **III** | Validated Model | Peer-reviewed models with documented assumptions, validation against observations, uncertainty quantification | Projections, attribution, scenarios |
| **IV** | Expert Assessment | Consensus statements from scientific bodies (IPCC, NAS), systematic reviews, meta-analyses | Context, synthesis, confidence calibration |
| **V** | Qualified Report | Industry reporting under regulatory requirement, investigative journalism with named sources, legal proceedings | Supplementary, cross-reference required |
| **VI** | Contextual | Opinion, commentary, advocacy documents, anonymous sources | Background only, never as proof |

### Usage Rules

1. **Damage claims require Grade I-II evidence.** No exceptions.
2. **Causal pathway claims require Grade I-III evidence** with explicit uncertainty.
3. **Driver identification requires Grade II-V evidence** with multiple corroborating sources.
4. **Intervention assessments may use Grade IV-V evidence** with clear flagging.
5. **Grade VI material** provides context only and must be labeled as such.

## WORKFLOW: FROM OBSERVATION TO DOSSIER

### Phase 1: INTAKE (1-2 days)

**Objective:** Establish that the mechanism exists and matters.

1. Document the observed damage (what's dying, degrading, depleted)
2. Identify at least one Grade I-II source quantifying the damage
3. Sketch the suspected causal chain
4. Check for existing dossiers that might overlap
5. Draft a 1-paragraph executive summary

**Gate:** Does this mechanism represent a distinct, documentable pattern of human-caused damage? If yes, proceed.

### Phase 2: EVIDENCE GATHERING (1-2 weeks)

**Objective:** Build the evidentiary foundation.

1. Systematic search for all relevant datasets
2. Document data sources with quality assessment
3. Identify key studies, reports, official documents
4. Build initial bibliography with source notes
5. Flag critical knowledge gaps

**Gate:** Do we have sufficient evidence (Grade I-III) to document the mechanism with confidence? If yes, proceed.

### Phase 3: ANALYSIS (1-2 weeks)

**Objective:** Run the numbers, trace the pathways.

1. Data intake notebook: clean, validate, document
2. Analysis notebook: compute metrics, run scenarios, test sensitivity
3. Map the causal chain in detail
4. Identify feedback loops and enabling conditions
5. Document all assumptions explicitly

**Gate:** Can we reproduce all findings from documented code and data? If yes, proceed.

### Phase 4: ATTRIBUTION (1 week)

**Objective:** Identify who benefits, who decides, who could stop it.

1. Trace financial flows and beneficiaries
2. Map decision points and decision-makers
3. Document political economy (lobbying, capture, revolving doors)
4. Name specific actors where public record supports it
5. Assess intervention resistance

**Gate:** Can we explain why this mechanism persists despite known damage? If yes, proceed.

### Phase 5: SYNTHESIS (1 week)

**Objective:** Assemble the dossier and connect it to the network.

1. Write the full DOSSIER.md following template
2. Generate all visualizations with full provenance
3. Complete uncertainty register
4. Write intervention assessment
5. Map connections to other mechanisms
6. Internal review and revision

**Gate:** Does the dossier meet all quality standards? If yes, publish.

### Phase 6: MAINTENANCE (ongoing)

**Objective:** Keep dossiers current as evidence evolves.

1. Monitor for new data releases
2. Track policy or legal developments
3. Update findings when warranted
4. Log all changes in version history
5. Periodic full review (annual minimum)
