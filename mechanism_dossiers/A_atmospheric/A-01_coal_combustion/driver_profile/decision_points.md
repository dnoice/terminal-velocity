# Decision Points: A-01 - Coal Combustion Chain

## Executive Summary

The coal combustion chain involves thousands of discrete decisions across investment, operations, policy, and consumption domains. Key decision points with highest leverage include: new project investment decisions, plant retirement timing, grid dispatch rules, and policy/regulatory choices. Understanding who decides, using what criteria, enables targeted intervention.

---

## 1. Decision Point Inventory

### 1.1 Strategic Decisions (Long-term)

| Decision | Frequency | Time Horizon | Reversibility |
|----------|-----------|--------------|---------------|
| New mine development | Annual cycle | 20-40 years | Very low |
| New plant construction | Annual cycle | 30-50 years | Very low |
| Fleet retirement planning | 5-year cycles | 10-20 years | Medium |
| Corporate strategy/pivot | 3-5 years | Varies | Medium |
| National energy policy | Election cycles | 5-20 years | Medium |

| Decision | Frequency | Time Horizon | Reversibility |
|----------|-----------|--------------|---------------|

## 3. Plant Retirement Decisions

### 3.1 Retirement Decision Framework

| Factor | Pro-Retirement | Pro-Continuation |
|--------|---------------|------------------|
| Economics | Negative cash flow | Positive margin |
| Regulation | Costly compliance | Grandfather exemption |
| Market | Low dispatch priority | Capacity payments |
| Physical | Equipment failure | Remaining useful life |
| Policy | Phase-out mandate | Political protection |

### 3.2 Decision-Makers for Retirement

| Context | Decision-Maker | Key Consideration |
|---------|---------------|-------------------|
| Regulated utility | Utility + regulator | Rate recovery |
| Merchant plant | Owner/operator | Market conditions |
| State-owned | Government ministry | Employment, energy security |
| IPP | Project company | PPA terms, debt service |

### 3.3 Accelerating Retirement

| Intervention | Mechanism | Effectiveness |
|--------------|-----------|---------------|
| Carbon price | Increase operating cost | High |
| Renewable competition | Reduce dispatch | High |
| Efficiency standards | Force compliance investment | Medium |
| Retirement mandates | Regulatory deadline | High |
| Buy-out programs | Compensation for closure | High |
| Litigation | Force closure | Medium |

## 4. Grid Dispatch Decisions

### 4.1 Dispatch Decision Process

| Time Frame | Decision Type | Decision-Maker |
|------------|--------------|----------------|
| Day-ahead | Unit commitment | System operator |
| Intraday | Economic dispatch | System operator |
| Real-time | Balancing | System operator |
| Seasonal | Maintenance planning | Utility + operator |

### 4.2 Dispatch Criteria (Traditional)

| Criterion | Weight | Effect on Coal |
|-----------|--------|---------------|
| Marginal cost | Primary | Favors coal (low fuel cost) |
| Must-run status | High | Protects coal baseload |
| Ramp rate | Medium | Favors coal (slow but steady) |
| Reliability | High | Favors coal (historical) |

### 4.3 Shifting Dispatch Decisions

| Intervention | Effect | Status |
|--------------|--------|--------|
| Carbon pricing | Raises coal marginal cost | Partial implementation |
| Renewable priority dispatch | Coal dispatched last | Many jurisdictions |
| Storage deployment | Removes baseload need | Accelerating |
| Demand response | Reduces peak need | Growing |
| Grid modernization | Enables flexible dispatch | Underway |
