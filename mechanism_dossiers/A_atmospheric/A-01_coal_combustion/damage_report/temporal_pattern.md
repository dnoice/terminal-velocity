# Temporal Pattern: A-01 - Coal Combustion Chain

## Executive Summary

Coal's 250-year reign as industrial civilization's primary energy source is entering its terminal phase in developed economies while simultaneously peaking in developing nations. The temporal pattern reveals a dangerous lag between scientific understanding (1970s), policy response (2010s), and actual phase-out (2030s-2050s), during which cumulative damage continues to compound.

---

## 1. Historical Timeline

### 1.1 Pre-Industrial to Industrial Revolution (Pre-1750 to 1850)

| Period | Development | Impact |
|--------|-------------|--------|
| Pre-1750 | Surface coal for local heating | Minimal, localized |
| 1712 | Newcomen steam engine | Deep mining enabled |
| 1769 | Watt steam engine | Industrial scale-up |
| 1800-1850 | Railway expansion | Coal logistics revolution |
| 1850 | Global production: ~100 Mt | Atmospheric loading begins |

### 1.2 First Coal Age (1850-1950)

| Year | Milestone | Scale |
|------|-----------|-------|
| 1850 | UK dominates global production | 60% of world output |
| 1882 | First coal power plant (Edison) | Pearl Street Station, NYC |
| 1900 | Global production: 700 Mt | Britain, Germany, US lead |
| 1913 | Pre-WWI peak (UK) | 287 Mt (UK alone) |
| 1950 | Global production: 1,500 Mt | Post-war reconstruction |

### 1.3 Second Coal Age (1950-2000)

| Decade | Key Development | Production Level |
|--------|-----------------|------------------|
| 1950s | Post-war electrification | 2,000 Mt |
| 1960s | Oil competition begins | Growth slows in West |
| 1970s | First oil crisis, coal revival | Environmental awareness grows |
| 1980s | Acid rain crisis, SO2 controls | Scrubbers mandated |
| 1990s | China's economic rise | Shift to Asia begins |
| 2000 | Global production: 4,700 Mt | China surpasses US |

### 1.4 Third Coal Age - Peak Era (2000-Present)

| Year | Milestone | Scale |
|------|-----------|-------|
| 2000 | China joins WTO | Coal boom accelerates |
| 2007 | Global coal use: 6,400 Mt | Historic acceleration |
| 2013 | Global peak? (IEA initial estimate) | 8,265 Mt |
| 2014 | US coal decline accelerates | Shale gas competition |
| 2015 | Paris Agreement | 1.5¬∞C target adopted |
| 2020 | COVID-19 disruption | Temporary 4% decline |
| 2022 | Energy crisis spike | New record: 8,500+ Mt |
| 2023 | Post-crisis stabilization | 8,800 Mt (new peak) |
| 2024 | China expansion continues | Structural peak uncertain |
