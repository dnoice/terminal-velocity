# Leverage Points: A-01 - Coal Combustion Chain

## Executive Summary

Using Donella Meadows' hierarchy of leverage points, this analysis identifies where interventions in the coal combustion system can be most effective. The highest leverage points involve changing the paradigm (coal = progress) and system goals (growth â†’ wellbeing), while lower but more immediately actionable points include financial flows, information flows, and physical infrastructure.

## 2. High Leverage Points

### 2.1 Paradigm Shift (Rank 2)

**Current Paradigm:** Coal represents progress, industrial strength, energy security

**Target Paradigm:** Coal represents legacy technology, health threat, stranded asset risk

| Intervention | Mechanism | Status |
|--------------|-----------|--------|
| Reframe coal as obsolete | Narrative change | Emerging in developed countries |
| Highlight clean energy success | Demonstration effect | Accelerating |
| Coal = illness framing | Public health messaging | Underutilized |
| Stranded asset discourse | Financial risk framing | Growing |

**Effectiveness:** Very high if achieved, but slow and difficult

### 2.2 System Goals (Rank 3)

**Current Goal:** Maximize electricity supply at minimum cost (ignoring externalities)

**Target Goal:** Maximize wellbeing while minimizing total societal cost

| Intervention | Mechanism | Implementation |
|--------------|-----------|----------------|
| Wellbeing metrics | Alternative to GDP | Bhutan GNH, New Zealand |
| Full cost accounting | Include externalities | SCC adoption |
| Just transition framing | Equity as goal | ILO framework |
| Health-centered planning | Health impact assessment | WHO guidelines |

### 2.3 System Rules (Rank 5)

**Key Rules to Change:**

| Current Rule | Target Rule | Mechanism |
|--------------|-------------|-----------|
| Free emissions | Emissions priced | Carbon pricing |
| Cheap coal allowed | Coal phase-out mandated | Regulation |
| Unlimited operation | Operating permits conditional | Enforcement |
| Subsidies permitted | Subsidies eliminated | Policy reform |
| Liability limited | Full liability | Legal reform |

### 3.2 Feedback Strength (Ranks 7-8)

**Strengthen Negative Feedback (Balancing Loops):**

| Feedback | Current Strength | Intervention | Target Strength |
|----------|------------------|--------------|-----------------|

| Health feedback | Delayed (chronic effects) | Acute awareness | Moderate |
| Political feedback | Weak (lobby capture) | Campaign finance reform | Moderate |
| Investment feedback | Emerging | ESG mandates | Strong |

| Infrastructure lock-in | Strong | Early retirement | Weak |

## 4. Lower Leverage Points

### 4.1 Physical Infrastructure (Rank 10)

**Stock-Flow Structure:**

| Infrastructure | Current Stock | Intervention | Timeline |
|----------------|---------------|--------------|----------|
| Coal plants | ~2,400 GW global | Early retirement | 2030-2050 |
| Coal mines | ~10,000 globally | Managed closure | 2030-2040 |
| Rail/port infrastructure | Massive | Repurposing | Gradual |
| Grid connections | Coal-optimized | Grid modernization | Ongoing |

### 4.2 Buffer Sizes (Rank 11)

| Buffer | Current State | Intervention |
|--------|---------------|--------------|
| Coal stockpiles | Strategic reserves | Phase down |
| Grid reserve margins | Coal-heavy | Storage substitution |
| Financial reserves | Limited transition funds | Just transition funds |

### 4.3 Parameters (Rank 12)

**Adjustable Parameters:**

| Parameter | Current | Target | Mechanism |
|-----------|---------|--------|-----------|
| Carbon tax rate | $0-150/t (varies) | $150+/t | Legislation |
| Renewable subsidy | $150B/year | $500B+/year | Budget |
| Coal subsidy | $7T+ (implicit) | $0 | Reform |
| Air quality standards | Varies | WHO guidelines | Regulation |
