# Source Reliability Assessment: A-01 - Coal Combustion Chain

## Reliability Grading Criteria

| Grade | Reliability | Criteria |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| A | Excellent | Peer-reviewed or rigorous government methodology, transparent, low bias risk |
| B | Good | Reputable institution, clear methodology, manageable bias |
| C | Acceptable | Useful data with caveats, requires triangulation |
| D | Limited | Significant bias risk or methodological concerns |
| F | Unreliable | Should not be used for primary claims |

---

## 1. Intergovernmental Organizations (IGOs)

### 1.1 International Energy Agency (IEA)

| Attribute | Assessment |
|-----------|------------|
| **Grade** | A |
| **Authority** | Premier energy data organization; OECD-affiliated |
| **Methodology** | Transparent, documented, peer-input |
| **Data Quality** | High; standardized collection from national agencies |
| **Update Frequency** | Annual (major); quarterly (selected data) |
| **Potential Bias** | Historically conservative on transition speed; improving |
| **Appropriate Use** | Energy production, consumption, emissions, projections |

## 6. Source Triangulation Matrix

### 6.1 Recommended Triangulation

| Data Type | Primary Source | Secondary Sources | Caution Sources |
|-----------|----------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Production/consumption | IEA | EIA, national statistics | Industry claims |
| Emissions | IEA, EDGAR | Global Carbon Project | Self-reported |
| Health impacts | GBD, WHO | Academic studies | Industry-funded |
| Economic costs | IMF, OECD | Academic | Industry |
| Climate science | IPCC | Academic journals | Contrarian |
| Plant/pipeline | Global Energy Monitor | IEA, national data | Company claims |
| Finance tracking | BankTrack, Reclaim Finance | Company reports | Industry PR |

### 6.2 Cross-Validation Requirements

| Claim Importance | Minimum Sources | Grade Requirement |
|------------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Core conclusions | 3+ | At least 2 Grade A |
| Supporting data | 2+ | At least 1 Grade A/B |
| Contextual | 1+ | Grade B minimum |

## 7. Known Source Limitations

### 7.1 Data Gaps

| Gap | Impact | Mitigation |
|-----|--------|------------|
| Artisanal/small-scale coal | Undercount production | Acknowledge in ranges |
| Developing country health data | Attribution uncertainty | Use GBD modeled estimates |
| Subsidy transparency | Undercount in some countries | Use IMF methodology |
| Company emissions | Scope 3 incomplete | Note limitations |

### 7.2 Methodological Debates

| Topic | Debate | Treatment |
|-------|--------|-----------|
| Social cost of carbon | Discount rate, damages | Report range |
| Value of statistical life | Transfer methods | Report range by region |
| PM2.5 threshold | Evidence for "no threshold" | Follow WHO consensus |
| Attribution | Individual vs. systemic | Use multiple approaches |
