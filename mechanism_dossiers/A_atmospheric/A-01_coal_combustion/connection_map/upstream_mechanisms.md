# Upstream Mechanisms: A-01 - Coal Combustion Chain

## Executive Summary

Coal combustion depends on multiple upstream mechanisms: coal mining and extraction, transportation infrastructure, financial flows, and enabling policy/regulatory frameworks. Interventions at upstream points can prevent combustion from occurring, often more effectively than downstream mitigation. This map identifies key upstream dependencies and intervention opportunities.

## 2. Extraction Mechanisms

### 2.1 Coal Mining Systems

| Mining Type | Global Share | Key Regions | Environmental Impact |
|-------------|--------------|-------------|---------------------|
| Surface (strip/mountaintop) | ~40% | USA (Appalachia), Australia, Indonesia | Landscape destruction, water contamination |
| Underground (longwall) | ~50% | China, Poland, Ukraine | Subsidence, methane release |
| Underground (room & pillar) | ~10% | India, South Africa | Worker safety, fires |

### 2.2 Extraction Dependencies

| Dependency | Description | Vulnerability |
|------------|-------------|---------------|
| Geological reserves | Fixed location, finite | Depletion, stranded resources |
| Water supply | Required for dust suppression, processing | Competing demands, scarcity |
| Energy inputs | Electricity for operations | Cost, availability |
| Labor force | Skilled mining workforce | Aging, declining recruitment |
| Equipment supply | Heavy machinery, replacement parts | Supply chain, capital |

### 2.3 Extraction Intervention Points

| Intervention | Mechanism | Effectiveness |
|--------------|-----------|---------------|
| Mining permit denial | Regulatory | High (prevents extraction) |
| Water rights restriction | Legal/regulatory | High in water-scarce regions |
| Mine rehabilitation bonds | Financial | Moderate (increases costs) |
| Methane regulations | Environmental | Moderate (affects economics) |
| Worker transition programs | Social | Enables political feasibility |

## 3. Transportation Mechanisms

### 3.1 Coal Logistics Systems

| Transport Mode | Global Role | Key Infrastructure |
|----------------|-------------|-------------------|
| Rail | Primary mover | Coal unit trains, dedicated lines |
| Maritime | Export/import | Bulk carriers, coal terminals |
| Road | Short-haul, smaller operations | Trucks, mine-to-rail |
| Conveyor | Mine-mouth operations | Direct mine-to-plant |

### 3.2 Transportation Dependencies

| Dependency | Description | Coal Specificity |
|------------|-------------|------------------|
| Rail infrastructure | Heavy-haul rail capacity | Often coal-dedicated |
| Port terminals | Bulk handling facilities | Coal-specific investments |
| Road networks | Mine access roads | Mixed use |
| Equipment | Locomotives, ships, trucks | Partially coal-specific |

### 3.3 Transportation Intervention Points

| Intervention | Mechanism | Examples |
|--------------|-----------|----------|
| Rail capacity denial | Regulatory | Block new coal rail lines |
| Port expansion opposition | Planning | Prevent terminal expansion |
| Rail divestment | Corporate | Rail companies exit coal |
| Maritime restrictions | Regulation | Port closures to coal ships |
| Infrastructure repurposing | Planning | Convert coal rail to other uses |

## 5. Policy/Regulatory Enablers

### 5.1 Enabling Policy Framework

| Policy Type | Function | Coal Dependency |
|-------------|----------|-----------------|
| Energy policy | Sets generation mix | Market rules favor coal |
| Mining policy | Governs extraction | Permitting, royalties |
| Environmental policy | Sets standards | Weak enforcement enables |
| Trade policy | Import/export rules | Tariffs, FTAs |
| Tax policy | Investment incentives | Subsidies, depreciation |

### 5.2 Regulatory Enablers

| Regulation | Function | Coal Impact |
|------------|----------|-------------|
| Mining permits | Authorizes extraction | Required for operations |
| Environmental approvals | Allows pollution | Weak limits enable |
| Air quality standards | Sets emission limits | Lenient standards favor coal |
| Water discharge permits | Allows effluent | Required for operations |
| Grid connection | Enables dispatch | Infrastructure access |

### 5.3 Policy Intervention Points

| Intervention | Mechanism | Impact |
|--------------|-----------|--------|
| Mining moratorium | Stop new permits | High (prevents expansion) |
| Stricter emissions standards | Regulation | Moderate (raises costs) |
| Carbon pricing | Market | High (affects economics) |
| Air quality enforcement | Regulatory | Moderate-High |
| Subsidy removal | Budget | Very High |

## 7. Upstream Intervention Effectiveness

### 7.1 Intervention Comparison

| Intervention Point | Effectiveness | Feasibility | Priority |
|-------------------|---------------|-------------|----------|
| Finance (new projects) | Very High | High (achieved in many cases) | 1 |
| Mining permits | Very High | Moderate | 2 |
| Transportation infrastructure | High | Moderate | 3 |
| Insurance | High | Moderate-High | 4 |
| Subsidies | Very High | Low (political) | 5 |

### 7.2 Upstream vs. Downstream

| Aspect | Upstream Intervention | Downstream Intervention |
|--------|----------------------|------------------------|
| Prevention | Stops harm before it occurs | Mitigates harm after |
| Lock-in | Prevents new lock-in | Deals with existing |
| Political | Fewer affected parties | More diffuse opposition |
| Effectiveness | Higher (source control) | Lower (end-of-pipe) |
| Visibility | Lower | Higher |
